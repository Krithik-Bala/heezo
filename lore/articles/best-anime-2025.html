Top 10 Anime of 2026 (So Far) That You're Sleeping On

📅 Jul 3, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read By Heezo

Alright, we're halfway through 2026 and the anime landscape is absolutely unhinged right now. Every season there's like 40 new shows, most of them are mid, but buried in that pile are genuine bangers that somehow nobody's talking about. I watched so you don't have to (okay I watched because I'm obsessed, but still).

This isn't a popularity contest. Jujutsu Kaisen and One Piece don't need your help. This list is for the shows that are quietly cooking while nobody pays attention. Let's get into it.

10. Hoshikuzu Telepath

A show about building a rocket to communicate with an alien? Sounds wild. But it's actually this gorgeous, quiet slice-of-life about found family and believing in something bigger than yourself. The animation from Studio Gokumi is absurdly pretty for what it is. If you liked A Place Further Than the Universe, this is your next obsession.

9. Kaiju No. 8: Season 2

The first season was good. The second season said "that was the tutorial." The power scaling goes nuts, the animation budget clearly doubled, and they finally let Kafka cook in ways the manga readers have been screaming about for years. Production I.G. understood the assignment.

8. Dandadan (Continued)

Listen, I know this technically started late last year, but it's still airing and it's still the most chaotic, beautifully animated show on television. Every episode is a speedrun through like five different genres. Science Saru is doing things with this animation that shouldn't be legal. If the first episode didn't hook you, I genuinely don't know what to tell you.

7. Lazarus

Shinichiro Watanabe (Cowboy Bebop, Samurai Champloo) came back from his nap and dropped a cyberpunk thriller about a world where a pharma company poisons humanity to sell the cure. It's got that Watanabe sauce — incredible music direction, stylish action, morally grey everything. The adult swim of it all. Chef's kiss.

6. Sakamoto Days

A retired hitman who now runs a convenience store. That premise alone sold me, but the execution is immaculate. The fight choreography is John Wick meets comedy manga, the animation switches between fluid sakuga and intentionally janky slapstick, and the ensemble cast grows on you like fungus (affectionate). TMS is handling this perfectly.

5. Blue Lock: Season 3

I know, I know — "sports anime." But Blue Lock isn't a sports anime. It's a psychological death game that happens to involve soccer. Season 3 throws the entire roster into international matches and the mind games are operating at a level that makes Death Note look like checkers. The animation improved significantly and the villain introductions go insanely hard.

4. Witch Hat Atelier

This was the manga that artists kept screaming about for years and the adaptation delivered. It's Harry Potter for adults who actually like art — a world where magic is literally drawn, and the visuals reflect that in every single frame. The color design alone deserves an award. Quiet, gorgeous, and deeply compelling worldbuilding.

3. Pluto (Season 2 Arc)

Naoki Urasawa's masterpiece continues with the back half dropping this year and it's some of the most emotionally devastating anime I've ever seen. A murder mystery in a world of robots and humans that asks what it means to be alive, told with the restraint and precision of a prestige HBO drama. Studio M2 understood that less is more. The silence in this show hits harder than any explosion.

2. Chainsaw Man: Part 2

Part 1 was iconic. Part 2 is unhinged in completely different ways. MAPPA threw out the rulebook — new protagonist energy, new visual language, and fights that feel genuinely dangerous again. The shift from Denji to the school arc hits different when animated. The opening alone had me calling the police because it's criminal how good it is.

1. The One That's Actually #1: Ruri Dragon

Nobody saw this coming. A girl wakes up with dragon horns and just... goes to school. That's it. That's the premise. And it's the most refreshing, genuine, quietly beautiful show of the year. It's what Slice of Life was supposed to be — no forced drama, no harem nonsense, just vibes and gorgeous direction. BONES picked this up and treated it like their baby. The backgrounds alone could be framed in a gallery. This is the show I text people about at 2 AM.

Honorable Mentions That Almost Made It

  • Re:Zero Season 3 (Continued) — Still peak suffering, still peak storytelling
  • Spy x Family: Movie — Does exactly what it needs to do, perfectly
  • Wind Breaker S2 — If you like punching with emotions, this is it
The point isn't to watch everything. The point is to watch the right things. One banger hits harder than ten mid shows you'll forget by next season.

That's the list. Half of these are going to blow up by year-end when the "best of 2026" lists drop and everyone pretends they were watching from day one. You're not everyone. You found them first.

Now go clear your watchlist. You've got work to do.
