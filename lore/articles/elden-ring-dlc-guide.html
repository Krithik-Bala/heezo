Elden Ring DLC: Every Secret You Missed (Trust Me)

📅 Jul 1, 2026 ⏱ 10 min read By Heezo

Look, I need to be honest with you. I've put over 200 hours into Shadow of the Erdtree and I'm still finding stuff. This DLC is not a DLC. It's a second game that FromSoftware decided to sell for $40 because they're either insanely generous or clinically unable to make something small. Either way, you missed things. Everyone missed things. Let me show you what you walked past.

Fair warning: spoilers ahead. If you haven't beaten the DLC yet, bookmark this and come back. Or don't. I'm not your dad.

The Shadow Realm Blessings You're Ignoring

First things first — the Scadutree Fragment system is not optional. I know some of you tried to muscle through the DLC at base power because "git gud" or whatever. Respect. But you're also fighting bosses that scale expecting you to have 10+ fragments by midgame. There are 50 total scattered across the Realm of Shadow, and most people finish with like 15.

Here's where to find the ones everyone misses:

  • The Cerulean Coast hidden cave — Behind the waterfall that looks decorative. It's not decorative.
  • Rauh Base, under the ruins — There's a destructible floor near the eastern grace. Roll into it. Yes, really.
  • The Specimen Storehouse, Floor 3 — Behind the painting. You need to attack the wall. Classic FromSoft.
  • Abyssal Woods entrance — The one guarded by those horrible hand-spider things. You know the ones.

Hidden Boss: The Putrescent Knight (Round 2)

You fought this thing in the Fissure. Congrats. But did you know there's a second encounter in the Darklight Catacombs that drops a completely different weapon? The Putrescent Cleaver is legitimately one of the best strength weapons in the game and most people don't know it exists because they cleared the Fissure and never went back underground.

To trigger it: beat the Fissure version, rest at a grace, then enter the Darklight Catacombs from the western entrance (not the main one). The boss fog appears where there was previously just a dead end.

The Backstitched Armor Set (Missable)

This is genuinely rage-inducing. There's a full armor set — the Backstitched Set — that you can only get by completing Hornsent's questline before entering the Specimen Storehouse. If you progress the main path too far, his quest auto-fails and the armor is locked forever on that playthrough. The set has the highest poise-to-weight ratio in the game.

FromSoftware designing quests that permanently fail if you breathe wrong will never not be insane behavior. But here we are.

The Lore Connection Nobody's Talking About

Okay, this is where it gets nerdy. The entire DLC is essentially a prequel story to the base game's Erdtree burning. Messmer — the hidden child of Marika — was sent to the Realm of Shadow to erase the hornsent civilization from history. The same way Marika shattered the Elden Ring, she shattered an entire people's existence. The base game's "hidden lore" items about the crucible and the primordial form of the Erdtree? They're all about THIS place.

The single most important item description in the DLC is on the Euporia (the twinblade you get from the final boss). Read it. Then re-read the description of the Erdtree's Favor talisman from the base game. Same event. Different perspective. Your brain will break.

Weapon Tier List (The Real One)

Forget what the subreddit says. After extensive testing, here's what actually slaps in PvE:

  1. Milady (Light Greatsword class) — The new weapon class is busted. The stance mixups are Sekiro-level satisfying.
  2. Euporia — Final boss weapon. Scales with faith and the weapon art does split damage that ignores like 40% of armor.
  3. Smithscript Cirque — Throwable axes that return. Sounds gimmicky. It's not. The DPS is obscene.
  4. Greatsword of Radahn (Light) — Not the colossal version. The light version has a weapon art that combos into itself for infinite poise damage.
  5. Ancient Meteoric Ore Greatsword — Hidden in Ruined Forge 4. Gravity damage + bleed. Yes, both.

The Secret Ending Path

This isn't technically a secret ending — the DLC doesn't add new endings to the base game. But there IS a final conversation with Miquella that changes based on whether you completed every NPC quest in the Realm of Shadow. If you did it all — Thiollier, Leda, Moore, Hornsent, Freyja, and the needle knight — Miquella's final dialogue acknowledges it, and you get an extra Remembrance option from Enia back in Roundtable Hold.

The extra Remembrance gives you a spell called Vow of the Alliance. It's a co-op buff that's essentially useless in single player but absolutely broken in multiplayer. +25% damage to all allies in range for 60 seconds. Speedrunners are going to love it.

Quick Tips You Probably Need

  • The Impenetrable Thorns spell hard-counters the final boss. It reflects his golden projectiles.
  • You can skip the Abyssal Woods stealth section entirely with a specific gesture (the one from Thiollier's quest).
  • Every furnace golem has a weak spot on its back left ankle. Three charged heavies. Done.
  • The DLC map has a second layer underground. Open your map and look for the toggle. I know people who beat the DLC without realizing this.
Shadow of the Erdtree isn't just the best DLC FromSoftware has ever made — it might be the best DLC anyone has ever made. And you still missed half of it. Go back in.
